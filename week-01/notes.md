# Week 1: Linux Fundamentals & Filesystem Navigation
During the initial week of Phase 1, the primary objective was mastering the Linux command-line interface (CLI) and understanding core permission structures. In the Terminal Velocity and Keymaster sessions, I navigated the Filesystem Hierarchy Standard (FHS) to locate hidden artifacts. A critical command utilized was `chmod 600`, which modifies octal permissions to enforce the Principle of Least Privilege by restricting file access strictly to the owner. Additionally, I employed stream editors like `grep` and `awk` to parse massive log files and extract threat actor IPs. These skills culminated in **TLAB-01: Operation Clean Sweep**, where I successfully executed a full threat hunt mission and extracted actionable intelligence directly from the terminal.
