QUESTIONS:
1. What Nmap flag did you use to find the specific software versions running on these ports?
Answer: I used the -sV flag, which completes the TCP handshake and interacts with the open port to grab the service banners, determining the exact software version.

2. Why is it dangerous to run an intense scan (like -A) on a fragile legacy system?
Answer: An intense scan (-A) sends thousands of aggressive, sometimes malformed packets and executes heavy enumeration scripts. On older, fragile legacy systems, this sudden flood of unexpected traffic can overwhelm limited CPU/memory resources, causing the service to crash and resulting in an accidental Denial of Service (DoS).
